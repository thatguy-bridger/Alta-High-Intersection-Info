Why signals increase costs
Signals add rear-end crashes — drivers going 35 mph face sudden red lights
Right-turning drivers look left for gaps, missing pedestrians already in the crosswalk
Left-turners watch oncoming traffic, not the crosswalk ahead of them
Signals still have 32+ conflict points — same as a stop sign
This intersection meets zero of 7 federal warrants for a signal
Why roundabouts work here
Deflection curves physically force drivers to slow — can't blow through like a stop sign
Only 8 conflict points vs. 32 → 75% fewer chances for serious crashes
88–90% reduction in serious injury and fatal crashes (national data)
Continuous flow = less idling, 37% emissions reduction next to a school
Grant already secured — city match (~$300K) equals the cost of a signal
Additional safety features recommended for school-adjacent roundabout
Pedestrian fencing

Channels students to marked crosswalks rather than crossing freely when school lets out

RRFBs at crossings

Rectangular rapid flashing beacons activate when pedestrians press to cross, visible to drivers

Z-crossings

Median islands force pedestrians to physically turn and face traffic before completing the crossing

Deflection curves

Approach geometry slows every vehicle entering the roundabout — no option to proceed at speed

Advance warning signs

Roundabout-ahead signage on 110th South (downhill) where the intersection isn't visible until late

Crossing guards retained

This intersection already qualifies; crossing guards would remain in place post-construction

"When I started this petition, the primary goal was safety reform — prioritizing pedestrian safety first. High school students, elementary students, people walking around. Looking at the comparison, there's only one option highlighted green for pedestrian safety. That makes the decision pretty easy."

— Marci, petition organizer and committee member

Committee recommendation: roundabout. The data supports it on every safety, operational, and cost measure. A grant is already in place. The city's out-of-pocket cost is the same as installing a signal.
